Color Types

Branding Colors

A color palette is used in branding to describe the marketing purpose of an element:

primary
The most prominent color of the brand. This color is usually featured in site-level elements such as the masthead and footer. It may also be used among a series of buttons to encourage the primary action.
secondary
A less prominent core color of the brand. This color is usually used as the background for page-level elements such as a left navigation bar or right search box.
tertiary
An offset color of the brand. This color is usually used as the background of tangentially-related elements such as asides and supporting content. It may also be used for pull outs.
neutral
A brand color that carries no prominence connotation or semantic meaning.
default
The default color used for text and borders throughout the site, carrying no particular semantic meaning or prominence.
inverse
A color meant to represent the inverse of default, carrying no particular semantic meaning or prominence.

Mood Colors

success
Indicates positive content or interactions, such as success messages, online badges and create buttons.
warning
Indicates potentially negative content or an interaction that may lead to negative outcome.
danger
Indicates dangerous content or interaction the user should be wary of, such as delete success messages and a delete button.
error
Indicates content about an error, such as a failed operation message.
info
Indicates informational content or interactions, such as a helpful message or a control that presents more info.
highlight
Indicates information or interactions that should be highlighted in prominence.
important
Indicates information or interactions that should not be ignored.
required
Indicates information or interactions that must not be ignored, such as a required control.
